Home

The Xbox Controller is a widely used handheld device for console and PC gaming. It features a hybrid D-Pad for precise input, textured grip on the bumpers, back, and triggers, and low-latency wireless technology for a more responsive experience. The Xbox Accessible app allows users to customize button mapping to adapt to their playstyle and create a unique experience. Available in a wide range of colors, the controller supports Bluetooth connectivity and USB-C wired play. It will allow you to configure the controller for Xbox or PC gaming. For easy access to different sections, the links to the sections are provided at the top of the page.

User Guides

To connect your Xbox controller wirelessly, press and hold the Pair button on your console until the light starts flashing. Then, press and hold the Pair button on your controller until the Xbox button begins to flash rapidly. Once both lights stay solid, your controller is successfully connected.

For PC users, you can connect via Bluetooth, a USB-C cable, or the Xbox Wireless Adapter for Windows. Make sure your device's Bluetooth is turned on and that you select “Xbox Wireless Controller” from the list of available devices.

FAQs

  • How do I connect my contoller?: Press the pair button on your console and hold down the pair button on your controller.
  • What batteries does it take?: Two AA batteries.
  • How do I update my controller?: Connected to your console, head to the "Xbox Accessories" app and install updates.
  • What is low-latency wireless?: low-latency wireless makes your controller respond faster by reducing the delay from button presses. This is a feature automatically included on newer controllers.
  • Can I custom map my buttons?: Yes, there are settings in the "Xbox Accessories" app.
  • Can I use it on other devices?: Yes, it also works on PCs and some smart phones.
  • Can I connect my headset?: Yes, you can plug it into the 3.5mm jack on the bottom of the controller

Troubleshooting

  • Controller won't turn on: First, check if the batteries are correct or charged. If batteries aren't the problem, try to connect the controller using a cable. If that still doesn't work, try resetting your controller by holding the Xbox button for 10 seconds.
  • Input is delayed: Remove any objects that may be interfering. Update your controller in the "Xbox Accessories" app. If there are still issues, connect the controller via a USB-C cable
  • Buttons aren't working: Restart your console and check for any dirt around the buttons. Use a soft cloth or a cotton swab to clean around the buttons. If issues still occur, you may need to replace the controller.
  • Battery dies quickly: Turn off the controller when not in use. Update firmware in the "Xbox Accessories" app and reduce vibration intensity. If the battery is still weak, change the batteries used or use a rechargeable battery.
